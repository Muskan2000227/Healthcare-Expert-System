BMI

What is BMR?

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic physiological functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production at rest.

  • Calculation is influenced by factors like age, sex, weight, height, and body composition.

  • Purpose: Helps determine daily calorie needs for weight management, considering activity levels.

Low BMR Risks

  • Slower Metabolism: A low BMR indicates a slower metabolism, meaning the body burns fewer calories at rest.

  • Weight Gain Risk: Individuals with a low BMR may be more prone to weight gain because their body requires fewer calories to maintain basic functions.

  • Low Energy Levels: People with a low BMR might experience lower energy levels, leading to fatigue and difficulty staying active.

  • Difficulty Losing Weight: Those with a low BMR may find it challenging to lose weight, even with diet and exercise.

High BMR Risks

  • Faster Metabolism: A high BMR indicates a faster metabolism, meaning the body burns more calories at rest.

  • Weight Loss or Difficulty Gaining Weight: Individuals with a high BMR may struggle to gain weight or maintain weight because their body requires more calories to function.

  • Higher Energy Levels: People with a high BMR often have more energy and may find it easier to stay active.

  • Nutritional Needs: A higher BMR can increase nutritional requirements, making it essential to consume enough calories and nutrients to support metabolic demands.
